The rite of the duel

Five beats from a called premise to a settled match, every ruling adjudicated under validator consensus on GenLayer.

  1. beat 1

    Call the duel

    P1 names a premise and sets best of 3 or 5. The match opens with a single empty corner.

  2. beat 2

    A challenger answers

    The first wallet to take the right corner becomes P2 and the match goes live.

  3. beat 3

    Seal in secret

    Each corner commits a concealed tactic. Neither move is shown until both are in.

  4. beat 4

    The Referee adjudicates

    Validators each re-judge the clash. They must agree on the winner exactly, with the margin within tolerance.

  5. beat 5

    The match advances

    A near-even clash is forced to a draw. First corner to clinch the majority wins it all.

A note on secrecy: sealed moves are hidden from every contract view until both corners commit, but raw chain state is technically readable on this testnet. Treat it as a public arena, not a vault. This is a documented testnet limitation.
